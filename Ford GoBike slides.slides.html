(Bike Ridership and Influencing Factors in San Francisco in 2017 )¶

by: Bassam Faramawi¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of bike ridership in San Francisco in the 2nd half of 2017 and the factors that could influence it. The main focus was on the five factors: Trip Duration, Member Age, Member Gender, User Type and time of trip in terms of Month, WeekDay and Hour of day.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consisted of trip durations and attributes of approximately 519,700 bike trips. The attributes included duration_sec, member_birth_year, member_gender, user_type and start_time&end_time features as well as additional measurements such as bike_id, start_station_id, and end_station_id.

Distribution of Trip Durations¶

Trip durations in the dataset take on a large range of values, from about a 1 minute trip to about 1400 minute at the longest duration. Plotted on a logarithmic scale, the distribution of durations prices takes on a normal distribution shape with a spike of short durations between 6 and 20 minutes durations.

Customers and Subscribers activity during Day Hours¶

Giving the expected activity of trips at rush hours(darkest points), there is much activity in the subscribers side at this hours more than the customers side. In the early hours of the morning and afternoon, subscribers ride the bike on a daily basis more than rest of the day. This may be understood to be due to the reliance on the bike to some subscribers as a means of going to work and back.

Overall Ridership by Month¶

Biking ridership begin to increase in a monthly manner with summer months until the end of the summer (September) and then begin to decrease thereafter with autumn months. This may be due to the nature of the weather.

Trip Duration Vs. Member Age¶

It is clear that the relationship between the age of the members and duration of the trip is negative. The older the age, the shorter the trip and vice versa. It is noted that there is an intensity in short trips less than 20 minutes and also there is a density of trips among the young people under the age of 40 years.
